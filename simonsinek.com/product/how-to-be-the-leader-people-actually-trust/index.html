How to Be the Leader People Actually Trust (And Maybe Even Love)

Zach Mercurio with Simon Sinek

  • Instructor: Zach Mercurio with Simon Sinek
  • Course Length: ~2 Hours (34 bite-size modules, ~4 minutes each)
  • Skills: Leadership, Communication, Inspiration, Creativity, Confidence

Good news: To be a great leader, you dont need innate charisma, a larger-than-life personality, or to channel your inner Steve Jobs. According to renowned leadership expert Zach Mercurio, Ph.D., the most effective leaders have mastered a set of simple, teachable psychological skills that energize and inspire others to contribute.

In this course, youll learn how to make others feel seen, heard, valued, and motivated to take action. Youll unlock a psychological toolkit to master the art of micro-interactions, implement effective delegation strategies, cultivate a sense of significance, and more.

About Your Instructors

Zach Mercurio

Zach Mercurio works with individuals and organizations worldwide to forge purposeful leaders who cultivate more meaning, mattering, well-being, and performance. He is a positive leadership and organizations researcher and Honorary Fellow of Psychology in the Center for Meaning and Purpose at Colorado State University. He is also the author ofThe Power of Mattering: How Leaders Can Create a Culture of SignificanceandThe Invisible Leader. His research has been awarded by the Association for Talent Development and The Academy of Management, and he contributes regularly to international media outlets.

Read More About Zach

Simon Sinek

Simon Sinek is an unshakeable optimist and the founder of The Optimism Company. He believes in a bright future and our ability to build it together. Simon may be best known for his TED Talk on the concept of WHY, which has been viewed over 70 million times, and his video on millennials in the workplacewhich reached 80 million views in its first week and has gone on to be seen hundreds of millions of times.

He continues to share inspiration through his bestselling books, including global bestseller Start with WHY and New York Times bestsellers Leaders Eat Last and The Infinite Game, as well as his podcast, A Bit of Optimism.
